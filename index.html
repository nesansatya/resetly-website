01
The problem

We lived the problem
before we solved it.

We weren't athletes. We were regular people who kept downloading fitness apps, feeling instantly overwhelmed, and quitting by day three. Every single time.

Every app we tried assumed we were already motivated. They were built for the version of us we wanted to be — not the version that existed on a Tuesday night after a long day of work, scrolling through a 47-exercise programme wondering where to even begin.

The gym was expensive. The personal trainers were more expensive. And every "free" app had a paywall on the third screen. We were tired of it. So we decided to do something about it.

02
The research

The answer wasn't
willpower. It was friction.

We spent months understanding why people quit. Not why they start — that part is easy. Everyone starts. The question was why they stop, and why they always stop at the same point.

The pattern was clear. Too many features. Too much guilt when you miss a day. No personalisation for bad days. Apps that respond to "I'm exhausted and low" with "here's your 45-minute HIIT session."

"What if the app could read how you feel today — and actually respond to that instead of just ignoring it?"

That question became the entire product philosophy. Every feature in Weekly Reset was built to answer it.

03
The build

From blank screen
to live app. One session.

We built Weekly Reset from scratch — zero to live web app, Android APK, and payment processing — in a single extended session. No agency. No massive team. Just relentless focus and a clear vision.

Every decision was weighed against one question: does this make it easier or harder to quit? If a feature added friction, it was cut. We do things right from the start — or we don't do them at all.

Phase 1 — The free version
Full web app with dashboard, habits, mood coaching, water tracker, meal guide and progress screen.
Phase 2 — Monetisation
Stripe payments, Pro features, custom workout builder, extended history and billing portal.
Phase 3 — Android app
Native Android APK via Capacitor. The web app became a real app on a real phone.
04
Who it's for

Not for athletes.
For the rest of us.

We built this for people aged 25 to 35 who are budget-conscious, have previously tried and failed at building health routines, and want to feel better — not become an athlete.

The person who opens the app on a bad day and needs to be told that one 10-minute walk is enough. That person is who every single decision was made for.

05
Why it exists

Wellness shouldn't need
a membership fee.

We believe the baseline of feeling healthy — moving your body, drinking enough water, sleeping properly, eating reasonably — should be accessible to everyone. Not just people who can afford a gym or a personal trainer.

Motivation shouldn't be a prerequisite either. Most people aren't unmotivated — they're overwhelmed. Give someone a simple structure, meet them on their bad days, and watch what happens.

"One 10-minute walk on a bad day is worth more than a perfect week you never started."

The philosophy behind every Weekly Reset feature.
